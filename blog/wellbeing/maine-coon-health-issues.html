Genetic Conditions

Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)

Most common heart condition in Maine Coons. Regular cardiac screening is essential.

Hip Dysplasia

Joint condition affecting mobility. Maintain healthy weight and provide joint supplements.

Spinal Muscular Atrophy

Rare genetic condition affecting muscle development. DNA testing available.

Heart Health

HCM Prevention

  • Regular cardiac screening (echo/ultrasound)
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Low-sodium diet
  • Regular exercise

Warning Signs

  • Labored breathing
  • Lethargy
  • Loss of appetite
  • Sudden collapse

Joint Health

Weight Management

Maintain healthy weight to reduce joint stress. Regular exercise and controlled diet.

Supplements

Glucosamine and chondroitin supplements for joint health.

Environment

Provide ramps and steps for easy access to high places.

Prevention Strategies

Regular Check-ups

Annual veterinary exams and recommended screenings for early detection.

Diet & Nutrition

High-quality diet with appropriate protein levels and essential nutrients.

Exercise

Regular play and activity to maintain healthy weight and muscle tone.

Early Warning Signs

Physical Signs

  • Changes in appetite or water intake
  • Weight loss or gain
  • Changes in grooming habits
  • Limping or difficulty moving

Behavioral Signs

  • Increased lethargy
  • Changes in litter box habits
  • Hiding or unusual behavior
  • Vocalization changes
