Maine Coon Health Guide

Maine Coons are generally healthy cats, but they can be prone to certain health conditions. This guide covers common health issues, preventive care, and maintaining your Maine Coon's wellbeing.

Common Health Issues

Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)

  • Most common heart disease in cats
  • Regular cardiac screening recommended
  • Early detection is crucial

Hip Dysplasia

  • More common in larger breeds
  • Can cause mobility issues
  • Regular exercise helps prevention

Spinal Muscular Atrophy

  • Genetic condition
  • Affects muscle development
  • Testing available for breeding cats

Preventive Care

Regular Check-ups

  • Annual veterinary visits
  • Dental examinations
  • Weight monitoring

Vaccinations

  • Core vaccines
  • Regular boosters
  • Travel requirements

Parasite Prevention

  • Flea and tick control
  • Regular deworming
  • Heartworm prevention

Signs of Good Health

  • Clear, bright eyes
  • Clean ears
  • Healthy coat
  • Good appetite
  • Regular bathroom habits
  • Normal activity level

Emergency Signs

  • Difficulty breathing
  • Sudden weight loss
  • Lethargy or weakness
  • Loss of appetite
  • Vomiting or diarrhea
  • Changes in behavior
